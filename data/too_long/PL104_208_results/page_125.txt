PUBLIC LAW 104-208—SEPT. 30, 1996         110 STAT. 3009–124

Fund (UNICEF) pursuant to section 301 of the Foreign Assistance Act of 1961.

DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANCE

(INCLUDING TRANSFER OF FUNDS)

For necessary expenses to carry out the provisions of sections
103 through 106 and chapter 10 of part I of the Foreign Assistance
Act of 1961, title V of the International Security and Development
Cooperation Act of 1980 (Public Law 96–533) and the provisions
of section 401 of the Foreign Assistance Act of 1969, $1,181,500,000,
to remain available until September 30, 1998: Provided, That of
the amount appropriated under this heading, up to $20,000,000
may be made available for the Inter-American Foundation and
shall be apportioned directly to that agency: Provided further, That
of the amount appropriated under this heading, up to $11,500,000
may be made available for the African Development Foundation
and shall be apportioned directly to that agency: Provided further,
That of the funds appropriated under title II of this Act that
are administered by the Agency for International Development and
made available for family planning assistance, not less than 65
percent shall be made available directly to the agency’s central
Office of Population and shall be programmed by that office for
family planning activities: Provided further, That of the funds
appropriated under this heading and under the heading “Child
Survival and Disease Programs Fund” that are made available
for carrying out the provisions of section 104(c)(2) of the Foreign
Assistance Act of 1961, such sums as necessary may be used to
administer the amount made available for such activities and for
costs incurred in providing training and technical assistance and
for the overall operational costs of such programs: Provided further,
That of the amount made available under this heading for activities
and programs conducted through private voluntary organizations,
not less than $40,000,000 shall be made available to private voluntary
organizations that implement development assistance programs,
of which not less than $6,000,000 shall be made available to
private voluntary organizations that carry out child survival, basic
education, and other health programs, including immunization
and oral rehydration programs, exclusive of funds provided under
any other heading in this Act: Provided further, That exclusive
of costs incurred for direct administrative expenses by the Agency
for International Development, not less than 50 percent of the
amounts made available for the purposes of chapter 1 of part
I of the Foreign Assistance Act of 1961 shall be made available
through contracts or grants with eligible private voluntary organizations
or other private nonprofit entities, and funds appropriated
under this heading may be made available to programs that generate
proceeds resulting from commodities procured with funds appropriated
under this heading: Provided further, That none of the
funds appropriated in this Act may be used for any activity that
is designed to influence the outcome of a political election in any
country: Provided further, That no funds made available under
this heading shall be used for abortion or involuntary sterilization
as a method of family planning or to motivate or coerce any
person to practice abortions: Provided further, That section 107
of the Foreign Assistance Act of 1961 and section 533 of Public
Law 103-306 shall continue to be applicable to any level of assistance
percentages insufficient to recognize the total effort required: Provided
further, That funds appropriated under this heading should be
made available to combat and provide economic adjustments
for conflicts caused by ethnic violence.
